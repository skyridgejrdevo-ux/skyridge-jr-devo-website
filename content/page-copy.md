### About the Team

Skyridge Junior Devo (Devo) is the development mountain bike team for Skyridge High
School in Lehi, Utah. We're a non-profit program open to all current 7th and 8th grade
students — no experience required.

We compete as part of the Utah NICA (National Interscholastic Cycling Association)
league, which emphasizes inclusivity, sportsmanship, and personal growth as much as it
does racing. Our motto: **Strong, Brave, Kind.**

The program is entirely volunteer-led by certified NICA coaches and supported by
generous local sponsors and families.

---

### Eligibility & Requirements

**Who can ride?**
Any student currently enrolled in 7th or 8th grade who:
- Will be enrolled at Skyridge High School in Fall 2026, OR
- Is currently at a feeder school (Viewpoint, Lehi Junior High, etc.)

**Physical requirements:**
- Must be able to ride at least 5 miles on flat terrain before the season starts
- A basic trail/mountain bike in working condition (no road bikes or full-suspension
  required — but a cross-country hardtail or trail bike is ideal)
- Helmet (MIPS recommended), gloves, and appropriate trail clothing

**Academic requirements:**
- Maintain a 2.0 GPA or higher (per NICA rules)
- No eligibility issues per school/district guidelines

---

### Fees & Costs

**Team fee: $290 — includes Racing Kit (jersey, bib, t-shirt, hat, hoodie & socks)**
Covers coaching, equipment maintenance, insurance, team expenses, and Racing Kit.

**NICA registration: ~$90**
Paid directly to NICA through PitZone. Required for all racers.

**Race entry fees:** Included in NICA registration.

**Total estimated cost: ~$380 per athlete**

*Financial assistance is available — contact a coach if cost is a barrier.*

### What Parents Can Expect

**Communication:**
All team communication happens through Spond. Download the app and enable notifications.
Coaches also send email updates for important deadlines.

**Volunteer hours:**
Devo is entirely volunteer-run. Families are expected to contribute at races
(marshaling, tent setup, timing). Specific roles are assigned through Spond each race.

**Race day:**
Arrive 2 hours before your rider's start time. Devo start time is **2:30 PM** for all
races. Plan to stay through the awards ceremony.

**Rides leaders aren't babysitters:**
Rides start on time. Riders must be ready and signed in before the start. Coaches
enforce a 2:1 rider-to-leader ratio for safety — please respect this policy.

---

### Team Culture & Expectations

**Strong, Brave, Kind** — this is more than a motto. We expect riders to:
- Support teammates, especially those struggling on the trail
- Ride within their ability, ask for help, and be honest about limits
- Treat competitors, volunteers, and trail users with respect
- Leave no trace at every ride location

**No-drop rides:**
We do not leave riders behind. Faster riders are expected to wait and support
those still coming through. Speed will come — character first.

---

### FAQ

**Q: Does my rider need to be a strong cyclist to join?**
A: No. Devo is a development program. If your rider can ride 5 miles on flat ground,
we'll build from there. Many riders have never competed before.

**Q: What bike do we need?**
A: A trail or mountain bike with working gears and brakes. 24" or 26" wheels are fine
for most 7th/8th graders. No full-suspension required. We recommend a basic hardtail.
Avoid road bikes, cruisers, or bikes with thick knobby tires designed for dirt jumps.

**Q: What if my rider gets injured?**
A: Safety is our top priority. All rides are led by NICA-certified ride leaders.
Coaches carry basic first aid supplies on every ride. Riders are covered by NICA's
accident insurance during official team activities.

**Q: Do we need to attend every practice?**
A: Consistent attendance is strongly encouraged — it's how riders improve and bond with
teammates. That said, life happens. Just communicate through Spond if your rider will
be absent.

**Q: Can my rider participate if they have a school conflict?**
A: Talk to the head coach. We work with student-athletes involved in other sports and
activities where possible.

**Q: Is there a wait list?**
A: We cap the team based on the number of certified ride leaders available. Register
early. If we reach capacity, we'll maintain a wait list.

## PAGE 5: RIDE LEADERS

### Hero
**Headline:** Become a Ride Leader

**Subheadline:**
Our program only works because of volunteers. Ride leaders are the backbone of Devo —
they make safe, structured, fun rides possible every week.

---

### Why Volunteer?

You don't have to be a competitive cyclist to be a ride leader. You need to:
- Love riding bikes
- Be reliable and safety-conscious
- Enjoy mentoring young athletes

In return, you get to ride amazing trails, watch kids grow, and be a core part of a
team community. Most ride leaders say it's the best part of their week.

---

### NICA Certification Levels

NICA offers three certification levels. All are earned through online coursework and
on-bike training.

---

**Level 1 — Ride Leader**
The entry-level certification. Required to lead rides independently.

- Minimum 2-day in-person training
- Background check required
- Covers: group riding skills, risk management, trail etiquette, first aid basics
- Valid for 2 years

*This is what most parent volunteers pursue.*

---

**Level 2 — Head Coach**
Allows you to lead a team, organize practices, and manage race-day operations.

- Requires active Level 1 certification
- Additional in-person training
- Deeper coaching skills, athlete development, team management

---

**Level 3 — Expert Coach**
Advanced coaching, mentoring other coaches, team-building leadership.

*Rare in devo programs — most teams have 1–2 Level 3 coaches.*

---

### Rider-to-Leader Ratio

NICA requires a **minimum 6:1 rider-to-ride-leader ratio** for all on-trail activities.
In practice, we aim for 4:1 or better.

This means we need more ride leaders as the team grows. If we don't have enough
certified leaders for a ride, riders can't participate — safety first, always.

---

### How to Get Certified

1. Express interest — email skyridgejrdevo@gmail.com or talk to a coach
2. Register at [nationalmtb.org/coaches](https://www.nationalmtb.org/coaches)
3. Complete the online coursework (~4–6 hours)
4. Attend an in-person training (dates posted by Utah NICA)
5. Pass background check
6. Start riding with the team!

Certification costs are typically reimbursed by the team for new volunteers — ask a coach.

### Key Rules Highlights

**Helmet policy:**
A properly fitted, CPSC-certified helmet must be worn at all times while riding during
team activities. No exceptions.

**Sportsmanship:**
Taunting, disrespectful behavior toward competitors, coaches, or race officials may
result in disqualification or suspension from team activities.

**Trail etiquette:**
Yield to hikers and equestrians. Announce yourself before passing. Respect trail
closures — no exceptions.

**Communication:**
If your rider can't attend a practice or race, notify your coach through Spond in
advance. Repeated no-shows without communication may affect ride eligibility.

**Social media:**
Riders represent Skyridge Junior Devo online. Conduct on social media is held to
the same standard as in-person behavior.
